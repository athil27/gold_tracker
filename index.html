REFERENCE

NRI gold duty-free allowance (India) ▾

Under India's Baggage Rules 2026, for Indian passport holders/NRIs who've stayed abroad continuously for 1+ year, arriving with gold jewellery (not bars/coins):

  • Male traveller: 20 grams duty-free
  • Female traveller: 40 grams duty-free
  • Children with 1+ year abroad: same limits as above, by gender

Beyond that, and for gold bars/coins (which get no duty-free allowance at all), a concessional customs duty of roughly 6% applies if you've been abroad 6+ months — up to 1kg total per passenger, must be declared at the Red Channel. Shorter stays abroad lose the concessional rate and pay a much higher standard duty.

Rules and rates change with each Union Budget — verify current figures at cbic.gov.in or the ATITHI app before travelling with jewellery near these limits.

BACKUP

Everything above (purchases, goal, price history, settings) lives only in this browser's local storage. Export it periodically so you don't lose it if you clear browser data or switch devices.
Base data: gold-api.com (live XAU spot) and open.er-api.com (USD→INR/SAR rates). 22K uses 91.67% purity of 24K. The "local premium" settings above add India's import duty/GST and Saudi dealer margin on top of spot so the number matches real market rates like Google's — adjust those % if your local rate still doesn't line up; jewellers also add making charges on top, which this can't predict.

For popups to fire every 2 hours reliably, keep this app installed to your home screen and open/backgrounded (not force-closed). Android may still delay background timers on some phones — if that happens, disable battery optimization for this app/browser in phone settings.
