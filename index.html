Reference

NRI gold duty-free allowance (India) ▾

Under India's Baggage Rules 2026, for Indian passport holders/NRIs who've stayed abroad continuously for 1+ year, arriving with gold jewellery (not bars/coins):

  • Male traveller: 20 grams duty-free
  • Female traveller: 40 grams duty-free

Beyond that, and for gold bars/coins (no duty-free allowance at all), a concessional customs duty of roughly 6% applies if abroad 6+ months — up to 1kg per passenger, must be declared at the Red Channel.

Rules change with each Union Budget — verify at cbic.gov.in before travelling with jewellery near these limits.

Karat purity reference ▾

18K = 75.0% pure gold · 21K = 87.5% · 22K = 91.67% · 24K = 99.9% (effectively pure).

Spot vs retail vs jeweller price — what's the difference? ▾

Spot is the international bullion trading price. Local retail estimate adds your country's typical import duty/GST/dealer margin on top. Jeweller estimate adds a typical making charge on top of that — closest to what you'd actually pay in a store, though individual jewellers vary.
